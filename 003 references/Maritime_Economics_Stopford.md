# Maritime Economics av Martin Stopford, 3rd Edition

## Bokbeskrivelse
For 5000 years shipping has served the world economy and today it provides a sophisticated transport service to every part of the globe. Yet despite its economic complexity, shipping retains much of the competitive cut and thrust of the "perfect" market of classical economics. This blend of sophisticated logistics and larger than life entrepreneurs makes it a unique case study of classical economics in a modern setting.

The enlarged and substantially rewritten Maritime Economics uses historical and theoretical analysis as the framework for a practical explanation of how shipping works today. Whilst retaining the structure of the second edition, its scope is widened to include:
- lessons from 5000 years of commercial shipping history
- shipping cycles back to 1741, with a year by year commentary
- updated chapters on markets; shipping costs; accounts; ship finance and a new chapter on the return on capital
- new chapters on the geography of sea trade; trade theory and specialised cargoes
- updated chapters on the merchant fleet shipbuilding, recycling and the regulatory regime
- a much revised chapter on the challenges and pitfalls of forecasting.

With over 800 pages, 200 illustrations, maps, technical drawings and tables Maritime Economics is the shipping industry's most comprehensive text and reference source, whilst remaining as one reviewer put it "a very readable book".

## Forfatter
Martin Stopford has enjoyed a distinguished career in the shipping industry as Director of Business Development with British Shipbuilders, Global Shipping Economist with the Chase Manhattan Bank N.A., Chief Executive of Lloyds Maritime Information Services; Managing Director of Clarkson Research Services and an executive Director of Clarksons PLC. He lectures regularly at Cambridge Academy of Transport and is a Visiting Professor at Cass Business School, Dalian Maritime University and Copenhagen Business School.

## Detaljert synopsis

### Part 1: Introduction to Shipping
Addresses the questions of where shipping has come from and where it is now.

- **Chapter 1: Sea Transport and the Global Economy**  
  Shipping's 5,000-year history provides unique perspective on economic mechanisms. Discusses the 'Westline' path of sea trade centers and current trade in Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.

- **Chapter 2: The Economic Organization of the Shipping Market**  
  Overview of the market: transport system, demand, merchant fleet, transport provision, ports, company organization, and political influences.

### Part 2: Shipping Market Economics
Sets out the macroeconomic structure of the shipping market, focusing on market cycles, driving forces, and commercial environment.

- **Chapter 3: Shipping Market Cycles**  
  Characteristics of shipping cycles, explanations, 22 cycles since 1741 with accounts of each, economic mechanisms, secular trends, return on capital, and cycle prediction.

- **Chapter 4: Supply, Demand and Freight Rates**  
  Economic model of shipping market: supply, demand, freight rates. Ten key variables influencing supply/demand, market dynamics, and freight rate mechanisms.

- **Chapter 5: The Four Shipping Markets**  
  How markets work: commodities, freight, secondhand ships, new ships, demolition. Practicalities, cashflow connections, and shipowner behavior.

### Part 3: Shipping Company Economics
Microeconomics: costs, revenues, financing, and return on investment for firms.

- **Chapter 6: Costs, Revenue and Financial Performance**  
  Operating costs (voyage, operating), capital costs, company accounts (income statement, balance sheet, cashflow), and cashflow analysis.

- **Chapter 7: Financing Ships and Shipping Companies**  
  Historical financing methods, world capital markets, four main ways: equity, debt, new-building finance, leasing.

- **Chapter 8: Risk, Return and Shipping Company Economics**  
  Investment model, theory of the firm applied to shipping, determinants of return on investment, and risk pricing in shipping.

### Part 4: Seaborne Trade and Transport Systems
Focus on cargo and transport systems: geography, trade theory, bulk, specialized, and liner shipping.

- **Chapter 9: The Geography of Maritime Trade**  
  Physical dimensions: oceans, distances, transit times, trading network, and trade of major economic regions.

- **Chapter 10: The Theory of Maritime Trade**  
  Why countries trade, trade theory explanations, supply-demand model for commodities, evidence from 105 countries, trade development cycle, and relationship to economic development.

- **Chapter 11: Bulk Cargo and the Economics of Bulk Shipping**  
  Principles of bulk transport, commodities shipped in bulk, economic characteristics, and transport systems.

- **Chapter 12: The Transport of Specialized Cargoes**  
  Segments for specialized cargoes: chemicals, liquefied gas, refrigerated cargo, unit labour cargoes, passenger shipping.

- **Chapter 13: The Economics of Liner Shipping**  
  Containerization innovation, liner system organization, demand characteristics, and complex economic framework.

### Part 5: The Merchant Fleet and Transport Supply
Fleet of vessels, shipbuilding/demolition, regulatory framework.

- **Chapter 14: The Ships that Supply the Transport**  
  Ship design evolution: objectives (cargo containment, efficiency, cost), categories (liner, liquid bulk, dry bulk, specialist, service vessels).

- **Chapter 15: The Economics of Merchant Shipbuilding and Scrapping**  
  Location of shipbuilding capacity, market cycles in production/prices, economic principles, technology, ship scrapping.

- **Chapter 16: The Regulation of the Maritime Industry**  
  Regulatory institutions: classification societies (technical safety), flag states (laws), coastal states (environmental policing).

### Part 6: Forecasting and Planning
Analysis and forecasting for decision-making.

- **Chapter 17: Maritime Forecasting and Market Research**  
  'Forecasting paradox', types (market forecasts vs. research), techniques, common errors.
